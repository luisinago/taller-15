Seasonal color analysis is a method that categorizes individuals into distinct color palettes based on their natural features. It was originally developed by artists and color theorists to help individuals determine which colors best complement their skin tone, eye color, and hair shade.

The system divides colors into four primary seasons—Winter, Spring, Summer, and Autumn—and further refines them into 12 sub-categories. These categories help you identify colors that enhance your natural beauty and avoid shades that might dull or overwhelm your features.

The analysis is based on three key factors: undertone (warm or cool), intensity (soft or bright), and contrast (high or low). These characteristics combine to form your unique color profile.

For example, if you have a cool undertone with high contrast between your skin, hair, and eyes, you might belong to the Winter category. In contrast, if you have warm undertones and lower contrast, you could be classified under Autumn.

Knowing your season helps you create a cohesive wardrobe and makeup look, ensuring every piece you choose enhances your appearance.

Once you've determined your seasonal palette, you can start incorporating your best colors into every aspect of your style—clothing, accessories, makeup, and even hair color choices.

For example, if you're a Winter, you'll want to focus on cool, bold colors like black, white, and jewel tones. Avoid earth tones like orange or brown, as they might clash with your cool undertones.

If you're a Spring, embrace warm and light colors like coral, peach, and cream. Avoid overly dark or muted shades, as they can make your complexion appear washed out.

Having your personal palette gives you confidence in knowing that every outfit or makeup choice is tailored to bring out the best version of you.
